# 2026 FIFA World Cup

## Tournament Overview

- **Edition:** 23rd FIFA World Cup
- **Dates:** June 11 – July 19, 2026
- **Co-hosts:** United States (11 cities), Mexico (3 cities), Canada (2 cities)
- **Teams:** 48 (expanded from 32)
- **Total Matches:** 104 (increased from 64)
- **Groups:** 12 groups of 4 teams
- **Duration:** 39 days
- **Progression:** Top 2 per group + 8 best third-place teams → Round of 32

First World Cup with three co-host nations and first featuring 48 teams.

---

## Host Selection

The United 2026 bid defeated Morocco's bid with **134 votes vs. 65** at the June 13, 2018 vote in Moscow.

- **Mexico** becomes the first country to host three times (1970, 1986, 2026)
- **Canada** hosts for the first time
- **United States** previously hosted in 1994

## Tournament Format

- 12 groups of 4 teams
- Top 2 from each group + 8 best third-place finishers advance to Round of 32
- Previous proposed format (16 groups of 3) was rejected due to increased collusion risk
- Current 12-group format finalized March 14, 2023

---

## Draw

- **Date:** December 5, 2025
- **Venue:** Kennedy Center, Washington, D.C.

### Pot System

| Pot | Contents |
|-----|---------|
| Pot 1 | 3 hosts + top 9 ranked teams |
| Pots 2–4 | Remaining teams by ranking |
| Pot 4 | UEFA playoff winners + inter-confederation playoff winners |

### Seeding Rules

- Spain (ranked 1st) and Argentina (2nd) placed in opposite knockout bracket halves
- France (3rd) and England (4th) placed in opposite halves
- These pairs cannot meet until the final if both win their groups

## Qualification Highlights

- **4 World Cup debuts:** Cape Verde, Curaçao, Jordan, Uzbekistan
- **Qatar** qualified through competition for the first time (previously only as 2022 host)
- **Italy** failed to qualify — first former champion to miss three consecutive World Cups
- **Notable absences:** Denmark, Poland, Serbia, Wales, Cameroon, Costa Rica
- **Returns after long absences:** Norway, Austria, Scotland (last qualified 1998); Czech Republic (last qualified 2006); Turkey (last qualified 2002, finished 3rd)
- **Lowest ranked qualifier:** New Zealand (ranked 85th)

## Historical Notes

- Only **Estadio Azteca** (Mexico City) previously hosted World Cup matches (1970, 1986)
- No 1994 US World Cup stadiums are being reused
- 8 metro areas previously hosted tournament matches: Dallas, Los Angeles, San Francisco, New York/New Jersey, Boston, Guadalajara, Mexico City, Monterrey
